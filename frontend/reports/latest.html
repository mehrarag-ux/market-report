Three forces collided: (1) The SpaceX IPO triggered institutional cash-raising via selling of recent tech winners — particularly chip stocks — to fund both IPO allocations and post-listing index-weight positions, explaining why NVDA and AVGO were volatile despite eventually closing higher; (2) Iran peace-deal optimism drove a potential near-term collapse in Brent crude from above $100/bbl, which is bullish for inflation and therefore reduces pressure on the Fed ahead of the June 16–17 FOMC meeting under new chair Kevin Warsh; (3) The Nasdaq-100's quarterly rebalance announcement (effective June 22) added five new names — CoreWeave (CRWV), Astera Labs (ALAB), Nebius (NBIS), Rocket Lab (RKLB) and Teradyne (TER) — generating additional forced-buying flows and causing those stocks to surge after-hours.

For investors, the session's message is nuanced. The breadth reading (8 of 11 S&P sectors green) and the S&P trading +2.3% above its 50-day average are constructive, but the VIX at 22.22 — up +38% over the past week — signals that options markets are still pricing meaningful near-term risk. The Iran deal, if signed, could be a significant catalyst: Strait reopening would ease energy inflation, boost global growth expectations and give the Fed more flexibility to hold or cut rates. Conversely, a failure to sign or a hawkish surprise from Warsh at the FOMC could reprice the 10-year yield above 4.55% and pressure tech valuations. Retail investors in Singapore should be cautious about chasing Friday's spike — wait for clarity on Iran and the Fed before adding risk.
