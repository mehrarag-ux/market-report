SECTION 3 - Why Markets Moved

Wednesday's session opened with a tech hangover after a stellar Q2 close: investors dumped semiconductor names en masse, with Micron (MU) tumbling -10.57%, Intel (INTC) sinking -9.03%, and AMD dropping -6.89%, as profit-taking set in following a half-year in which chip stocks surged more than 80%. At the same time, the ADP National Employment Report showed private-sector job adds of only 98,000 in June — below the 110,000 consensus — adding uncertainty ahead of the critical non-farm payrolls release due today (Thursday, July 2). U.S.-Iran tensions also weighed at the margin, though WTI crude fell more than 4% to near $70/barrel as progress on Strait of Hormuz talks eased supply-disruption fears.

The session's two dominant forces pulled in opposite directions. First, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, speaking at the ECB's annual symposium in Sintra, Portugal, signalled that inflation risks have come down — providing a modest lift to rate-sensitive sectors including Financials and Communications. Alphabet (GOOGL) rose +1.07% and IBM +1.79%, while Microsoft (MSFT) gained +3.02%, reflecting a rotation from beaten-down hardware toward software and platform names that monetise AI without cyclical memory exposure. Second, post-Q2 profit-taking in the highest-flying semiconductor names was amplified by broader concern over whether the historic first-half AI-capex surge could sustain its pace; Micron had already reported record Q3 FY2026 results (revenue $41.46B vs. $35.84B est.) the prior week, meaning Wednesday's weakness was about valuation recalibration rather than fundamental deterioration.

For investors, Wednesday's action underlines two themes likely to dominate the next four to six weeks. First, sector rotation is accelerating: capital exiting overcrowded semis is flowing into Financials ahead of a Q2 earnings season kicking off July 14 with major banks, where FactSet consensus calls for 22% S&P 500 EPS growth. Second, SpaceX (SPCX) joining the Nasdaq-100 on July 7 — the fastest-ever NDX inclusion — will trigger an estimated $4.3 billion in passive inflows from QQQ and QQQM trackers, creating a near-term mechanical bid for the stock irrespective of fundamentals. Overseas investors should calibrate their tech exposure with awareness that the NDX is now -2.63% from its 52-week high even after a remarkable year, while macro tail risks from the July 28 FOMC decision and the ongoing U.S.-Iran diplomatic process remain the key binary events of the month.
