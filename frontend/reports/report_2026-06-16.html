SECTION 4 - Why Markets Moved

Tuesday June 16 delivered a bifurcated session: the Nasdaq 100 (NDX) dropped -1.9% as semiconductor and mega-cap tech names led the decline, with Intel (-8.45%), AMD (-7.3%), Micron (-6.18%), Broadcom (-4.37%), and Nvidia (-2.37%) all suffering notable losses. Citigroup (+1.26%), Alphabet (+1.06%), and SpaceX/SPCX (+4.83%) were notable exceptions, while the S&P 500 held up better at -0.6% due to Financials (+1.47%), Utilities (+0.72%), and Industrials (+0.65%) gaining ground on the day. The most active S&P 500 names confirmed the semiconductor stress, with Intel, Micron, and Nvidia among the heaviest-traded declining issues alongside Netflix (-4.56%) and Tesla (-1.50%).

The immediate catalyst is a confluence of macro and event-driven pressures. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's inaugural FOMC meeting on June 16–17 is the dominant overhang: markets heading into the week were pricing a 98.6% probability the Fed holds rates steady, but the critical unknown is the 'dot plot' — the March edition pencilled in one cut, but current conditions (headline PPI at +6.5% YoY, the highest since November 2022, and energy sub-index up ~37% YoY) make a hawkish revision likely with some policymakers potentially pencilling in a hike. Meanwhile, the US-Iran 60-day ceasefire deal — which sent oil below $80/barrel on Monday — has begun fading in its relief effect for tech multiples, as the focus shifts back to sticky inflation. Bank of America's pre-market-peak warning checklist hitting 70% — matching the historical average at seven prior major market tops — is adding an additional valuation overhang for growth stocks.

For overseas retail investors, the key takeaway is that this is a rotation pullback within an intact medium-term bull market rather than the beginning of a bear trend: the VIX at 16.2 is calm, 8 of 11 sectors remain green for the week, and the S&P 500 sits +3.3% above its 50-day moving average. The earnings season pivot point for the tech trade arrives June 24 when Micron reports Q3 — consensus expects $19.19 EPS — and a strong beat with positive demand commentary could re-ignite the semiconductor complex. However, investors should treat any near-term tech bounce with discipline given stretched valuations, the possibility of a hawkish dot plot, and the NDX trading near -2.3% below its 52-week high with a higher-rates-for-longer regime appearing increasingly likely.
