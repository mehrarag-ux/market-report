SECTION 4 - Why Markets Moved

Wednesday's session began positively as strong May retail sales (+0.9% vs. +0.5% expected) confirmed consumer resilience, and chip stocks attempted to recover from Tuesday's sharp profit-taking, which had seen Nvidia (-2.4%), Broadcom (-4.4%), Micron (-6.2%), AMD (-7.3%) and Intel (-8.5%) all post notable losses. Markets initially traded flat-to-higher ahead of the Fed's 2 p.m. ET decision, with the Dow coming within a hair of 52,000. However, after the FOMC statement dropped, stocks fell sharply as the updated dot-plot showed most officials aligning with the bond market's view that at least one rate hike is possible over the next 12 months — a stark pivot from March's projection of one cut.

The catalyst was Fed Chairman Kevin Warsh's press conference, at which he vowed to 'deliver price stability' and announced five structural task forces covering communications, balance sheet policy and other areas. Bank of America's pre-meeting survey showed 55% of respondents expected a hawkish tone, and CME FedWatch pegged the probability of at least one 2026 hike at 60%. The Iran-war energy shock — with Brent crude still above $100/barrel at its peak — has kept inflationary pressures 'stronger than expected for four months,' as one commentator put it, making the path of rate cuts politically and economically untenable. Ironically, markets partially recovered after Warsh's structural reform comments reassured investors on Fed independence.

For overseas investors, Wednesday's session reinforces that the US market's near-term trajectory is tethered to inflation data and Fed language rather than AI earnings momentum alone. The S&P 500 remains +1.7% above its 50-day moving average and VIX at 16.4 confirms no panic — correction risk is assessed as LOW — but zero of eleven sectors closed in positive territory, a dangerously narrow breadth reading. Technology and Communication Services led declines; the NDX quarterly rebalance on June 22 (adding ALAB, CRWV, NBIS, RKLB, TER) will create mechanical index-fund flows and could provide short-term support in those names. Investors should remain selective, hold quality AI infrastructure names with strong balance sheets, and watch the US-Iran deal signing (expected Friday) as a potential oil-price and inflation catalyst.
