SECTION 4 - Why Markets Moved

Monday June 22 opened on a mixed footing as markets processed the NDX quarterly rebalance — the first under Nasdaq's revised May 2026 membership methodology — which added five AI-infrastructure-oriented companies (Astera Labs, CoreWeave, Nebius, Rocket Lab, Teradyne) and removed Charter, Cognizant, Insmed, Verisk, and Zscaler effective at the open. Index funds were forced to mechanically sell excluded names and buy entrants, creating idiosyncratic flows that skewed sector performance away from pure fundamentals. Simultaneously, SpaceX (SPCX, -16.43%) announced a $20 billion bond issuance, triggering a sector-wide markdown of space and AI-adjacent hypergrowth names, with Alphabet (GOOGL) tumbling 4.99% on compounding AI capex concerns after reports that hyperscaler spending is accelerating faster than revenue conversion timelines. Energy prices fell on progress in U.S.-Iran peace talks, lifting Energy (+0.54%) and Industrials (+0.49%) while reducing geopolitical risk premium in the broader market.

The macro backdrop explains why the market's reaction was asymmetric rather than uniformly bearish. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's June 18 FOMC meeting left rates unchanged at 3.50–3.75% but the dot plot leaned hawkish, with nine officials projecting potential hikes and six projecting two or more 25bp increases in 2026 — a message that re-priced rate-sensitive growth stocks negatively. However, chip stocks bucked the trend: Micron (+6.82%), Intel (+5.19%), GlobalFoundries (+4.47%), and AMD (+2.65%) all extended gains as memory bulls positioned ahead of MU's June 24 earnings, where consensus calls for $34.66B in revenue and $19.95 EPS — figures that would have seemed impossible 18 months ago. The 10-year Treasury yield briefly touched 4.5% overnight, remaining elevated despite falling oil, further compressing the equity risk premium for long-duration growth names.

For overseas retail investors, the session's key takeaway is that the US market is in an advanced stage of AI-infrastructure differentiation: companies that directly manufacture or supply AI compute hardware (memory, custom silicon, networking ASICs) are holding or extending their momentum, while software-layer AI plays and communication services companies are facing a valuation re-rating as the market questions whether hyperscaler capex intensity will translate into near-term earnings. The NDX rebalance also signals index-level commitment to the AI-compute supply chain: the five new members (CoreWeave, Astera Labs, Nebius, Rocket Lab, Teradyne) all embody physical AI infrastructure themes. With PCE inflation data and a GDP third-estimate due Thursday, and MU earnings Wednesday after close, the next 72 hours will be the most event-dense of the month — market direction for Q3 could be materially set by Friday.
